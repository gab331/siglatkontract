Selected Work

Seismic Retrofit — Chlorine Warehouse, Mauban, Quezon

Five steel columns detached from their bases. Five more corroded to partial section loss. A chlorine storage facility in Seismic Zone 4 that was standing only because the roof diaphragm was redistributing loads the original design never anticipated. No as-built drawings. No site visit allowed before design. Emergency timeline.

We designed the complete retrofit: RC pedestal encasement with boot plate connections, knee brace lateral stiffening at 70° angle, 42 MPa high-early-strength concrete for schedule, and a phased construction sequence limiting work to two columns simultaneously. Every temporary bracing stage was designed for full gravity load plus a 2.0 dynamic impact factor. Anchor bolt connections verified per ACI 318-14 Chapter 17 with concrete breakout capacity confirmed at D/C = 0.39. Progressive collapse assessment identified the knee braces as providing an unintended but significant alternate load path for future column base deterioration — the same mechanism that caused the original failure. The structure was repaired without incident.

Client: Advanced Matrix Construction Inc. for Quezon Power (Philippines) Ltd.

Through-Truss Mezzanine System for Long-Span Column-Free Office Floors in Existing Steel Portal Frame Warehouses

A structural feasibility study investigating a novel framing concept for 30-meter clear span mezzanines inside existing portal frame warehouses. The through-truss system places the primary structure above the mezzanine floor — using the unused roof volume as structural depth — achieving vibration compliance per AISC Design Guide 11 at spans where conventional below-floor systems fail. Combined system natural frequency: 11.2 Hz. Peak walking acceleration: 0.005% g. Full member design per AISC 360-16, NSCP 2015, and ACI 318-14.

The Engineer

About

Siglat Konstract OPC is a one-engineer structural consultancy. The principal is Engr. Gabriel C. Mempin — licensed Civil Engineer (RCE) and Registered Master Plumber (RMP), with project experience spanning high-rise residential construction, industrial facility renovation, and emergency structural retrofit.

The firm operates on three principles that are not negotiable:

Engineering from first principles. We use ETABS, SAP2000, and every tool available. But we understand what the software is doing before we trust its output. The structural behavior governs the design — the software verifies it. When the model says something that contradicts the physics, we fix the model, not the physics. Current self-directed study includes Chopra's Dynamics of Structures and Clough & Penzien's Dynamics of Structures — because seismic retrofit demands understanding dynamics at the equation level, not just the button level.

Honest assessment, even when it's uncomfortable. We tell clients what the structure can and cannot do. We don't inflate capacity to win a project, and we don't add unnecessary conservatism to avoid thinking. If a building needs ₱5M in foundation work, we say that. If it needs ₱500K, we say that too. The numbers are what they are.

Construction-informed design. Every detail we draw has to survive contact with a real contractor, real weather, and real material availability in the Philippines. We design for the field, not for the plan checker's desk. If a connection can't be welded in position at 8 meters above the floor with a stick welder, we don't draw it.

Siglat Konstract works with a network of licensed architects, MEP engineers, and specialist consultants. When a project requires disciplines beyond structural engineering, we assemble the right team — but the structural work stays in-house, under our seal, with our name on it.

Based in the Philippines. Working within 100 km of Manila, with capacity for projects nationwide when the engineering demands it.
